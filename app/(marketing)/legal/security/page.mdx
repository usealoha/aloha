## Infrastructure

Aloha runs on Amazon Web Services in two primary regions:

- **us-east-1** (N. Virginia) — US customer data.
- **eu-west-1** (Ireland) — EU customer data.

Every service is deployed across multiple availability zones. The
primary database uses automated encrypted snapshots every hour;
point-in-time recovery is available for the last 35 days.

Static assets and edge routing run on Cloudflare, which terminates
TLS 1.3 at the edge and provides DDoS protection.

## Encryption

**In transit.** All external traffic is served over TLS 1.3. We
disable TLS 1.1 and below. HSTS is enabled with preload.

**At rest.** Production databases and object storage use AES-256
encryption via AWS KMS. Customer-managed keys are available on the
Enterprise plan.

**Secrets.** Platform secrets (OAuth tokens, webhook keys) are
encrypted with an envelope key rotated annually. Tokens for the
social networks you connect are encrypted per-workspace with a
unique key, so a single compromised record can't unlock another
customer's connections.

## Access controls

Production access inside Aloha follows the principle of least
privilege:

- **Role-based access.** Engineers get only the permissions their
  role requires; elevation requires a peer-reviewed request with a
  time limit.
- **MFA everywhere.** Hardware security keys for administrative
  access; TOTP minimum for everyone.
- **Single sign-on.** All internal systems behind SSO with
  mandatory device posture checks.
- **Audit logging.** Every production access event is logged and
  reviewed weekly.

No Aloha employee accesses Customer Data unless required to support
a specific ticket you've filed or to investigate a security
incident. Every such access is logged.

## Application security

- **Dependency scanning.** Dependabot + Snyk on every PR; critical
  CVEs patched within 24 hours, high within a week.
- **Static analysis.** CodeQL runs on every commit; secrets
  scanning blocks pushes.
- **Pen testing.** External third-party penetration test annually
  plus before any major architectural change.
- **Code review.** Two-person review required on every PR touching
  auth, billing, or customer data paths.

## Business continuity

- **Backups.** Hourly database snapshots, daily full backups
  shipped to a separate AWS account with restricted access.
- **Disaster recovery.** Quarterly DR tabletop exercises; RPO 1
  hour, RTO 4 hours.
- **Uptime.** Published at [/status](/status). 99.9% target on
  non-Enterprise plans; 99.95% on Enterprise with SLA credits.

## Compliance

- **SOC 2 Type II.** Current. Report available under NDA —
  request from
  <a href="mailto:security@aloha.social">security@aloha.social</a>.

  one click.
- **CCPA.** Covered by the [privacy policy](/legal/privacy); opt-

- **ISO 27001.** In progress; certification expected Q4 2026.

## Vulnerability disclosure

If you find a vulnerability, please report it privately to
<a href="mailto:security@aloha.social">security@aloha.social</a>.
Encrypt with our
[PGP key](https://aloha.social/.well-known/security.pgp) for anything
sensitive.

**What we promise:**

- We'll acknowledge receipt within 24 hours.
- We won't pursue legal action against good-faith research that
  stays within our scope and respects user data.
- We'll credit you publicly (with your permission) and pay bounties
  for qualifying reports — $250 to $10,000 depending on severity.

**Out of scope:** social engineering, denial-of-service attacks,
physical access attempts, and vulnerabilities in third-party
services we don't control.

## Incident response

Our incident response plan covers detection, containment,
eradication, recovery, and post-mortem. If a security incident
affects Customer Data, we notify affected customers within **48
hours** of awareness, per the [DPA](/legal/dpa).

Public post-mortems for significant incidents are published at
[/whats-new](/whats-new). We include root cause, timeline, impact,
and what changed as a result. We don't redact to look better.
